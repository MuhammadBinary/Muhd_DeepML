Reflecting on my ML journey and honestly it's emotional 🥹

I got interested in Machine Learning when I was around 12-13 years old. I remember sitting in a class at the Nigerian National Centre for Artificial Intelligence and Robotics, surrounded by adults(most of them graduates) And I didn't understand anything they were teaching. Like nothing. So I would put my head under the table and cry silently 😔 in a room full of grown people with degrees. And I still remember some of the women there laughing at me while they where coming back from the bathroom(graduates).

But I still didn't give up.

I was around 13-14 when I told myself I don't even want to become an ML engineer, I want to become an ML scientist and understand ML intuitively not just use it 🔬

I also took a Mathematics for ML course on edX, things like calculus, limits, differentiation and most of the time I would cry whenever I didn't understand something because it felt impossible and I kept asking myself "what if I just can't do this" 😢

Today I watched a video and one question just hit me:

💭 "Imagine if you gave up back then?"

Whenever you are in a journey, don't give up 🙏
